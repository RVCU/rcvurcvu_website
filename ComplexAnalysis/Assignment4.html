Using Cauchy's Integral Formula evaluate $$\int_0^{2 \pi} \frac{1}{2+sin \theta} d \theta$$

Letting $z = e^{i \theta}$ and $0 < \theta < 2 \pi$ We are going to try to go backwards to a line integral.
Recall $sin \theta = \frac{e^{i \theta - e^{-i \theta}}}{2i} \Rightarrow = \frac{1}{2i}(z-\frac{1}{z})$
