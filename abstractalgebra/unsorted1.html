Let F be a field and \(f \in F[x]\) a polynomial
a) Assume that F has characteristic zero. Prove that \(f'=0\) if and only if f is a constant polynomial
b) Assume that F has a prime characteristic p. Prove that \(f'=0\) if and only if \(f(x) = g(x^p)\) where \(g(x) \in F[x]\)

a) \(\Rightarrow\) let \(f'=0\) and suppose that \(deg(f)=n \geq 1\) then we must have that there is a term \(a_nx^n\) in f such that \(a_n \neq 0\). Therefore \(n \cdot a_nx^{n-1}\) is in f' and by assumption \(f'=0\) so \(n \cdot a_n = 0\). From this we can deduce $$n \cdot a_n = 0 $$ $$ n \cdot a_n \cdot 1 = 0 \cdot 1$$ $$ n \cdot 1 \cdot a_n = 0 $$ and by assumption \(a_n \neq 0\) so we get that \(n \cdot 1 = 0\) which means characteristic of F is not zero. A contradiction!
\( \Leftarrow \) let f be a constant polynomial. Then f' is cleary 0.

b) let \( f(x) = g(x^p) \) where p is the prime characteristic of the field F. So f looks like $$f(x)=g(x^p) = a_0 +a_1x^p +...+a_nx^{p \cdot n}$$ for some \(a_i \in F \) then finding the derivative we get that $$f(x)' = g'(x) = p \cdot a_1x^{p-1} + ... + n \cdot p a_nx^{n \cdot p -1}$$ So as each element in f' is multiplied by a multiple of p we get that \(f'=0 \)
